Casper H. Blaauw

Studying MSc Bioinformatics @ UCPH · Bioinformatics Student Assistant @ Clinical Microbiomics · Intern @ Winther group & Joshi group

I'm a bioinformatician, currently studying the MSc Bioinformatics at the University of Copenhagen and working as a part-time bioinformatician at Clinical Microbiomics. Besides this, I am a research intern working with Hiren Joshi and Ole Winther and I'm the president of CBioVikings, the Danish regional student group of the ISCB.

My main interest is understanding how systems come together to form a greater whole. These can range from subway systems to online communities, but my primary focus is understanding the rules behind the molecular mechanisms of life. This is what originally led me to study Molecular Life Sciences at Utrecht University, where the systems view I was looking for drove me to bioinformatics. The rest of my four years there were dedicated to biology, programming and statistics (including a minor in Applied Data Science), with a detour to the University of Hong Kong to study public policy and international relations. Alongside this, I worked in a variety of roles in education (from tutor to substitute teacher) for those four years.

Besides bioinformatics, I care about properly understanding and visualising data, science and technology's impact on society, and (inter)national public policy. In my free time, some of my passions are baking, cities, design, languages, and obscure geographical knowledge. To learn more about me, see my CV on LinkedIn, explore my code on GitHub, or scroll down for more details about my research and motivation.

Projects

Machine learning for microbiome analysis

Clinical Microbiomics

Built a ML pipeline to robustly link microbiome abundance with various phenotypes.

2021

Language modeling of proteins for glycosylation site prediction

University of Copenhagen

Used BERT-based language models, trained on UniProt rather than Wikipedia, to distill protein sequences to their core grammatical context in the language of life. Armed with the contextual representations for a variety of glycosylated sequences, I set out to figure out the rules behind O-GalNAc glycosylation, a biological process under complex multi-level regulation.

2021

Automating and standardising report creation

Clinical Microbiomics

Built a system to automate the production of production-level reports, integrating data and report text in RMarkdowns and exporting to fully-styled Microsoft Office documents.

2021

Dynamic eQTL mapping in development and stress

Utrecht University

Investigated the dynamics of transcriptional regulation during cellular stress and development. Incorporating this transcriptomic data with eQTL mapping provided new insights in the dynamics of transcriptomic regulation and increased eQTL detection accuracy.

2019-2020

Homomerisation state prediction using symmetry-driven protein docking

Utrecht University

Built a proof-of-concept pipeline to predict protein homomer stoichiometry from monomer structures using HADDOCK, an information-driven protein docking software.
